Learning Hub

This page organizes real learning resources into simple categories so visitors can build knowledge about wildlife conservation, biodiversity, ecosystem health, and sustainable action. Most of the links below come from official environmental organizations and open in a new tab.

Articles and Reports

These reading resources explain biodiversity clearly and connect the topic to conservation policy, species protection, and current global challenges.

The IUCN Red List of Threatened Species

Source: International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN)

This is one of the most important global resources for checking the conservation status of animals, plants, and fungi. It explains extinction risk and gives species-level information about habitat, threats, and conservation action.

Why it is useful: It helps students understand how conservation status is measured and why some species need urgent protection.

Visit Resource

What Is Biodiversity?

Source: World Wildlife Fund (WWF)

This explainer introduces biodiversity in a simple and readable way. It also explains why biodiversity matters to food, water, medicine, shelter, and ecosystem balance.

Why it is useful: It is a strong beginner resource for defining the topic in clear language before moving to more detailed reports.

Read More

UNEP and Biodiversity

Source: United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP)

This page explains biodiversity loss at a global scale and connects it to forests, oceans, climate, public health, and sustainable development. It also outlines how UNEP supports biodiversity policy and restoration work.

Why it is useful: It helps link wildlife conservation to wider environmental issues instead of treating it as a separate topic.

Visit Resource

Introduction to the Global Biodiversity Framework

Source: Convention on Biological Diversity (CBD)

This official page introduces the Kunming-Montreal Global Biodiversity Framework and explains its purpose, goals, and international importance. It shows how countries are expected to respond to biodiversity loss.

Why it is useful: It gives the policy background behind many wildlife conservation efforts discussed on this website.
